## Project Overview
This repository contains a detailed corporate finance evaluation of  
Infosys Ltd. conducted as part of an academic Corporate Finance project.

The analysis focuses on:
- Risk and return characteristics
- Financial performance and capital efficiency
- Cash flow strength and dividend policy
- Cost of capital and investment attractiveness

All analysis is consolidated in a single Excel workbook:  
`Infosys_Project.xlsx`

## Analysis Breakdown (Sheet-wise)

### 1. Stock Price & Risk Analysis
**Sheets Used:**  
- `RawPrice_Daily`  
- `RawPrice_Weekly`  
- `RawPrice_Monthly`  
- `Beta_Calculations`

**What was done:**
- Analyzed price movement across multiple time horizons  
- Computed returns and volatility  
- Estimated beta to measure systematic risk  

**Key Insight:**  
Infosys shows relatively low volatility and a stable beta, reinforcing its low-risk profile among IT services firms.

---

### 2. Financial Statements Analysis
**Sheets Used:**  
- `Income_Statement`  
- `Balance_Sheet`  
- `Cash_Flow_Statement`

**What was analyzed:**
- Revenue and margin trends  
- Asset-light balance sheet structure  
- Consistency of operating cash flows  

**Key Insight:**  
Strong profitability combined with minimal leverage highlights disciplined financial management.

---

### 3. Ratio Analysis
**Sheet Used:**  
- `Ratio_Analysis`

**Ratios Covered:**
- Profitability (ROE, ROCE, margins)  
- Liquidity ratios  
- Solvency ratios  

**Key Insight:**  
High returns are driven by operational efficiency rather than financial leverage.

---

### 4. Working Capital Management
**Sheet Used:**  
- `Working_Capital`

**Focus Areas:**
- Receivables efficiency  
- Payables management  
- Net working capital position  

**Key Insight:**  
Efficient working capital cycle supports strong free cash flow generation.

---

### 5. Cost of Capital
**Sheet Used:**  
- `WACC`

**Components:**
- Cost of equity (using CAPM)  
- Capital structure weights  
- Overall WACC estimation  

**Key Insight:**  
Low leverage results in a higher reliance on equity, increasing WACC slightly but reducing financial risk.

---

### 6. Cash Flow & Dividend Analysis
**Sheets Used:**  
- `Cash_Flow_Analysis`  
- `Dividend_Analysis`

**What was evaluated:**
- Free cash flow sustainability  
- Dividend payout consistency  
- Shareholder return policy  

**Key Insight:**  
Strong operating cash flows comfortably fund dividends without compromising reinvestment.

---

### 7. Investment Evaluation
**Sheet Used:**  
- `Investment_Analysis`

**Decision Factors:**
- Risk-return tradeoff  
- Financial stability  
- Cash flow visibility  

**Conclusion:**  
Infosys is positioned as a **low-risk, stable investment**, suitable for long-term investors rather than high-growth seekers.

## Key Learnings
- Stable cash flows can justify valuation premiums  
- Capital efficiency often matters more than aggressive expansion  
- Conservative leverage reduces risk but limits upside from financial leverage  
- Governance and predictability are critical in corporate valuation
